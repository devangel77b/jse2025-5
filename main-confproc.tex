\addtocontents{toc}{\emph{From the cover:} Electrical conductivity could potentially be used as a means to perform non-destructive testing (NDT) of conductive 3D printed parts such as those made from metal or from conductive filament. As 3D printing technology improves and such materials become more available, means of ensuring quality will be needed. Here we examine issues in using conductivity through 2D and 3D measurements as well as computational studies. \emph{Cover image: Tushaar Akula, Miguel Arenas, Cole Canada, Daivik Jajoo, Anton Lavrenov, and Timur Neyir.} \bigskip\\}
